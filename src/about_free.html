ABOUT

Free Step is a dance that consists of sliding on the floor movements with their legs and hands under the beats of electronic music. The dance is based on elaborate and quick movements, and to include other dance styles such as Melbourne Shuffle, Jumpstyle and C-Walk.

HISTORY

The Free Step is the evolution of ancient Rebolation , which is the evolution of another foreign dance called Charleston Dance (dance that move the legs, listening to a kind of blues as music), the Brazilian saw, he liked and adapted to electronic music creating rebolation. The dance turned fun between young and was very successful in Rave parties in Brazil by 2008 and 2009, following the Dance genre was based on the Psy Trance and rhythms. In late 2009, it saw the need to create new steps for the dance, as the Rebolation was very limited steps forward and back, there was not much variation. The Rebolation then underwent a makeover, making the most elaborate steps mainly by the use of hops in their own base. Also in late 2009, the band called Axe Music Parangolé created a song called Rebolation, inspired by dance, but implying that it was based on rebolados of ax and not the movement of the legs. The music was very successful in Brazil and with it the general association of the population was made between the Parangolé and Rebolation, constraining who said that danced Rebolation. The name "Free Step" was given by "dancers" of this type, and was very successful, especially the Internet. Today there are competitions called "Meet Up" in which dancers compete with each other, being judged for innovation, timing, perfection and creativity in the sequences.

KEY MOVEMENTS

  • Base: The base is the main movement of the Free Step. He crosses his legs forward, backward or sideways. Normally when you use the Base, the dancer tends to move in the direction in which the step is executed. With training develops the ability to make a base on the spot where it is. With more training still manages to make a base in any direction you want, but few achieve this feat.
  • Hand moves: The hand movements complement the steps with his legs, are usually inspired Tecktonik style.
  • Kicks: The kicks are used very often to increase base.
  • Spins: The spins (spins) are also widely used and in different ways, tend to have a more attractive visual effect but requires more experience of the dancer to use them.
  • Combos: Sequence of predefined free movements such as hand moves, kicks and spins, and may even enter footsteps of other dances

STYLES

Free Step exist in many styles, such as:

  • Basic Style: Consists of dance with the base and movements without breaking or mixtures thereof. The cleanest possible style of dance.
  • Kick Style: When you use up many diverse steps based on jumps and kicks.
  • Speed ​​Style: Consists of accelerated and short grassroots movements; Speed ​​is the focus. Used especially in music with more than 130 beats per minute the time between a major and another is very short.
  • Slide Style: Uses a foot steps which usually tends to remain fixed while the other is slid across the floor.
  • Joker Style: humorous intention movements, with interpretation and musicality. Used to make the audience laugh in competitions, ridicularizarando your opponent. All taking into account the Sports Spirit.
  • Robot Style: Uses movements of arms and legs in robotic style, pricipalmente based on the Street Dance Poppin style.
  • Agressive Style: Also known as Aggressive Mode is the most elaborate of the aspects of Free Step, uses extremely strong and impact moves. Due to the need for more speed and perfection in motion is the most complicated to dance.
